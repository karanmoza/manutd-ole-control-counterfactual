# Article Brief

## Recommended Thesis

Manchester United's 2021 summer strategy may have targeted the most glamorous weakness rather than the most important one. A control-oriented midfield signing likely would have improved the team's results, but not enough to erase the club's deeper structural flaws.

## Strong Headline Options

- Manchester United Solved the Wrong Problem in 2021
- What If United Had Bought Control Instead of Cristiano Ronaldo?
- Ronaldo or Midfield Control? Reframing United's 2021 Transfer Logic

## Core Claims The Project Supports

1. United's 2021/22 decline was reflected in both results and underlying process metrics.
2. A strong defensive-midfield profile would likely have improved points, especially by stabilizing the team's defensive profile.
3. The modeled improvement survives a mild/base/strong sensitivity check, which makes the conclusion more robust than a single-parameter thought experiment.
4. Even the stronger scenarios do not turn United into a dominant team, which implies the club's problems extended beyond a single transfer choice.

## Helpful Reality Anchors

- The "generic CDM" should be framed as a capability, not a mystery player.
- For readers who want real 2021 market examples, you can briefly mention players such as Manuel Locatelli or Boubakary Soumare as examples of control-oriented midfield profiles who were actually on the market in summer 2021.
- Casemiro should remain the secondary named scenario because he was signed later and is more exposed to hindsight bias.

## Claims To Avoid

- Ronaldo alone caused the collapse.
- One CDM would have turned United into title contenders.
- The simulation proves what definitely would have happened.

## Suggested Structure

1. Open with the decision question: what problem did United think they were solving?
2. Show the drop from 2020/21 to 2021/22 in both points and underlying indicators.
3. Explain why the squad's bigger need appears to have been control and defensive protection.
4. Introduce the counterfactual model and explain the assumptions in plain English.
5. Present the headline results: modest but real improvement with a CDM profile.
6. Show the sensitivity analysis so readers can see the conclusion is not hanging on one exact multiplier.
7. Conclude that United likely made a misaligned decision, but also that the club's problems ran deeper than one star signing.
