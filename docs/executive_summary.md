# Executive Summary

## Headline

Manchester United's 2021 decision problem was not primarily about adding more star power. It was about whether the squad needed more attack or more control.

This project models a counterfactual in which United do not sign Cristiano Ronaldo in summer 2021 and instead add a strong defensive-midfield profile. Using real match-level public data and a transparent scenario model, the project finds that the counterfactual improves results modestly rather than dramatically.

## Main Findings

- To Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's exit, United took `17` actual league points. The counterfactual CDM scenario produces a mean of `18.13` simulated points.
- Across the full `2021/22` league season, United finished on `58` actual points. The counterfactual CDM scenario produces a mean of `61.16` simulated points.
- The project suggests United likely solved the wrong problem in 2021, but also that the club's issues were broader than one transfer.

## Strategic Interpretation

- Ronaldo was not necessarily the sole cause of decline.
- But he appears to have been a misaligned solution relative to the team's greater need for midfield control and defensive protection.
- A better-fit signing likely helps the team profile, yet does not fully overcome the wider structural weaknesses visible across the season.

## Why This Matters

This is a squad-building and resource-allocation case, not just a football take. The project asks whether Manchester United misdiagnosed its biggest weakness and allocated capital toward the wrong capability.

That makes the project relevant to a broader management question:

When organizations face visible underperformance, do they invest in the most visible solution or the most structurally useful one?

## Interpretation Guardrails

- This is a counterfactual model, not a causal proof.
- The CDM is an archetype, not a one-player simulation.
- The value of the project lies in disciplined scenario analysis, transparent assumptions, and decision quality framing.
